Gig'o'Books DO's and DON'Ts

DO use Gig'o'Books IF:

  • You are doing something by yourself and you have simple affairs.
  • You want something that's tailored to your niche, is simple, and without unnecessary clutter.
  • You need to create tax-compliant invoices with a minimum of fuss.
  • You need to create regular tax reports for your local tax/revenue authority with a minimum of fuss.
  • You like things which are free or value for money.
  • You LOVE great customer support straight from the developers.
  • You resent artificial restrictions that push you into paying more money.
  • You don't want your data to be in the 'cloud' and locked-in by the vendor.
  • You want something that's open source and will keep working until you retire.
  • You need to handle multiple currencies.
  • You need double-entry accounting. (If you don't know what this is, then don't worry.)
  • You are privacy conscious.
  • You don't have employees (but having contractors is okay).
  • You don't have to keep track of inventory/stock (unless it's only a small amount).

DON'T use Gig'o'Books IF:

  • You have employees or you have to do payroll.
  • You have to keep track of inventory/stock (unless it's only a small amount).
  • You have complicated financial affairs.
  • Someone else works on the accounts at the same time as you.
  • You like spending money unnecessarily.
  • You like having to pay money monthly so your data doesn't get deleted, or
  • You like having to pay for updates every few years so you can continue to access your data.
  • You like having features that you never use.
  • You like having features that you don't understand.

Seriously, if you've read all the way down here, you should just try Gig'o'Books. Stop thinking about it and just do it. It's not like we've going to devour your brain or something.
